Seeing Winged Ants Inside Your Property

The appearance of winged ants inside your home or business can be a sign of a mature ant colony that is ready to swarm. Swarming is the process by which reproductive ants (winged males and females) leave the nest to mate and establish new colonies. Seeing winged ants indoors, particularly in large numbers, often indicates that a mature colony is nesting within your structure. This is a significant sign that requires immediate professional attention, as it means the infestation is well-established and capable of spreading. Differentiating between winged ants and termites can be challenging for the untrained eye, but it's crucial for correct identification and treatment. Winged ants have elbowed antennae, a constricted waist, and forewings that are longer than their hindwings. Termites, on the other hand, have straight antennae, a broad waist, and wings of equal length. If you see winged insects that you suspect might be ants or termites, it's best to contact a professional for accurate identification. Our technicians are experienced in identifying different winged insects and can quickly determine if you have a swarming ant problem or something more serious like termites.
